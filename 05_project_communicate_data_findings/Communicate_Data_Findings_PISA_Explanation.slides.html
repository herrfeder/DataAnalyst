Communicate Data Findings for PISA 2012¶

by David Lassig¶

Investigation Overview¶



This dataset shows the effort of the OECD giving reasonable test results to argue about academic performance of school children in dependency to many different factors. Everyone that has children knows this predicament. We want our kids to be very sucessful in their later lives but we don't want to leverage them to be more hardworking and disciplined as it could harm their emotional condition. The evaluation of this dataset could point out the right advice to give our kids the right settings for beeing happy and sucessful in their schooldays and their whole life at all. I will pick up some of the factors and show several coherences between them.

Dataset Overview¶



This dataset is huge as it holds 485490 rows by using 636 different columns for several tests. It includes results from questionaires regarding:

  • home environment of students
  • parental environment of students
  • condition of the school
  • the habits and practices of the teacher
  • test results from science-oriented tests
  • self-perception of the students

Therefore it's crucial to cut only a few interesting columns from this dataset. I've used this document to get a explanation for the partly cryptic column names. It's one of the codebooks next to some others that are exclusively designed for the purpose to explain the different columns.

Preparation of the Data¶



I did some steps to prepare the data for visualisation:

  • summarize multiple categorical columns into one numeric variable
  • creating subsets
  • regrouping and filtering for specific categoric values

Correlation of Main Features¶

To get a fast overview of the relations between my choosen main features I'm using a correlation matrix heatmap to easily point out:

  • there isn't any really strong correlation between a pair of variables
  • the Practices of Teachers influences the Interest and Motivation for Math but NOT the Self-Efficacy and Familiarity with Math
  • the Interest in Math influences the Math Self-Efficacy but NOT the Familiarity with Math
  • Wealth doesn't affect any variable at all

def df_row_normalize(dataframe):
    '''Normalizes the values of a given pandas.Dataframe by the total sum of each line.
    Algorithm based on https://stackoverflow.com/questions/26537878/pandas-sum-across-columns-and-divide-each-cell-from-that-value'''
    return dataframe.div(dataframe.sum(axis=1), axis=0)

Scatterplot to show Influence of Mothers Job on Wealth and Math Familiarity¶

Final Words¶



I'm glad that Wealth has only a small influence on the mathematical achievements of a student as it's a variable that is very uneven distributed in the world.

Otherwise it depends strongly on the nation you're born into, whether a student is prone to strong mathematical performance.

Finally, the parents can influence their students achievements by the job they choose. I'm sure the choice of job is only a representation of behaviour, hobbies and interest the parents have. Therefore it's more the behaviour behind the job title that shapes our children to become better math performers.
